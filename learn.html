Success-Based Pilot Pilot

Instead of paying monthly, you can structure an engagement where payouts happen only after outcomes (revenue, milestones, or agreed triggers).

Find expert help

How it works

  • Choose an outcome trigger: first revenue, revenue threshold, milestone completion, or custom terms.
  • Agree on a fee model: success fee (one-time), revenue share (ongoing), or milestone-based.
  • Track outcomes: record revenue/milestones and trigger payouts once conditions are met.

Important: Templates below are for product scaffolding only and are not legal advice. Use counsel for anything binding.

Template: Success Fee (one-time)

Good for “pay when it works.”

Phase 1 Resources

Quick, opinionated checklists you can use immediately.

1) Validation (first 7 days)

  • Write your ICP and top 3 pains (one sentence each).
  • Run 10 interviews; measure pain intensity and willingness-to-pay.
  • Draft a landing page + waitlist; test 2 acquisition channels.

2) Planning (next 7–14 days)

  • Define MVP scope (what you will NOT build).
  • Choose a pricing hypothesis (one metric, one price point).
  • Pick 3 success metrics: activation, retention, revenue.

3) Build (weeks 3–4)

  • Ship onboarding first (time-to-value < 5 minutes).
  • Instrument events: signup, activation, key action, retention.
  • Launch a small pilot (5–20 users) and iterate weekly.
